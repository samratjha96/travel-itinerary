Why July works — and where most guides get it wrong

Everyone says "avoid Vietnam in July, it's rainy season." That misses the point. The central coast — Nha Trang, Quy Nhon, Hoi An — sits in near-dry season in July, with 7–9 hours of sunshine daily. Nha Trang is surrounded by mountains that physically block the monsoon: less than 10mm of rain the entire month. This itinerary stays in that window the whole time.

The Destinations

Five Places Worth the Journey

🌿 Jungle

Cat Tien
National Park

Lam Dong Province · Nights 2–3

Vietnam's most celebrated lowland rainforest, 3 hours from Saigon. In 2025 it received four international ecotourism awards — the only Vietnamese park to do so. This is where the gibbons are.

Dawn gibbon tracking — yellow-cheeked crested gibbons, critically endangered
Endangered Primate Rescue Centre, Crocodile Lake boat trip
Night safari jeep tour — the jungle comes alive after dark
Getting There3 hr car from HCMC
Stay2 nights
Night Safari~$10 pp
🏖️ Beach

Nha Trang

Khanh Hoa Province · Nights 4–5

Vietnam's diving capital — and arguably its best July beach destination. Mountains surrounding the bay block the monsoon so completely that Nha Trang sees less than 10mm of rain the entire month.

Hon Mun island dive or snorkel — Vietnam's best accessible coral reef
Doc Let beach — 18km of white sand, 30 min north, almost no one there
Fresh seafood straight from the fishing boats at Dam Market
Getting There1 hr flight from HCMC
Stay2 nights
Dive Trip~$25–35 pp
🏖️ Beach

Quy Nhon
& Ky Co Beach

Binh Dinh Province · Nights 6–8

TripAdvisor's 4th most trending destination on earth for 2026 — and still blissfully uncrowded. Ky Co beach is accessible only by boat: turquoise water, limestone cliffs, zero development. The window won't stay open long.

Ky Co — boat-access only, limestone cliffs, impossibly clear water
Bai Xep fishing village — photographers' paradise at dawn
800-year-old Cham towers at Duong Long, barely visited
Getting There3 hr bus from Nha Trang
Stay3 nights
Ky Co Boat~$5 return
🏖️ Beach + Town

Hoi An &
An Bang Beach

Quang Nam Province · Nights 9–10

The UNESCO Ancient Town is still magnificent — visit at dawn or after 6pm to dodge the crowds. The real base is An Bang Beach: 10 minutes by bike, quieter than My Khe, with good restaurants right on the sand. A perfect way to end a trip.

An Bang — the calmer, less touristy beach alternative to My Khe
Ancient Town by bike at dawn — lanterns, canals, nobody around yet
Cao Lau noodles and White Rose dumplings — dishes unique to Hoi An
Getting There3.5 hr bus from Quy Nhon
Stay2 nights (A) · 1 night (B)
Bike Hire$2/day
🕳️ Caves + Jungle Option B only

Phong Nha
& Hang En

Quang Binh Province · Night 10–11

UNESCO World Heritage karst — home to Son Doong, the world's largest cave. Son Doong itself is booked through 2027, but Hang En (3rd largest, bookable) lets you camp overnight inside the cave beside a river. Nothing else in Southeast Asia compares.

Dark Cave — zip-line into the entrance, then swim and kayak through it
Hang En overnight — camp inside a cathedral cave beside an underground river
Paradise Cave — 31km long, towering stalactite chambers
Getting There3.5 hr bus from Hoi An
Stay1–2 nights
Dark Cave~$20 pp
Hang En Trek~$200 pp

Before You Go

Book These in Advance

Most of this trip can be arranged on arrival. These four things can't.

⚡ Book Early
Cat Tien Eco-Lodge
Limited rooms inside the park. Night safari slots fill fast, especially on weekends.
⚡ Book Early
Flights — HCMC to Nha Trang
July is peak domestic season. Vietjet and Vietnam Airlines prices spike close to departure.
⚡ Book Very Early
Hang En Cave Trek
Oxalis Adventure manages all Phong Nha permits. Limited slots — book 3–4 months ahead minimum.
ℹ️ Worth Knowing
Son Doong Cave
The world's largest cave is fully booked through 2027. Only 1,000 permits per year exist. Hang En is the comparable alternative.
